Lettering and Modular Type Exploration

This project investigates systematic typographic structure, the minutia of legibility, the power of variations in form and the meaning they hold. Through this letterform exploration, I learned to leverage limited and controlled systems, integrate unexpected conditions, develop an appreciation for the influence of tools, and an eye for craft. The two appraoches were: typeface construction and lettering. The purpose of the two modes was to exaggerate the differences between the two.

Modular Type: letterforms as system Use consistent structures, recognizable build pattern between characters, a system thatâ€™s evident. It should act as a study for a full alphabet design. Letterforms are one color, no shading, vector.

Lettering: word as image, letterform as picture The word may act illustratively, pictorially, or use materials and textures which communicate as strongly in metaphor as the written meaning of the word. Final word does not need consistent structure between letters.
